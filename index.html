RF Battle Bots

One of our ADVANCED ROBOTICS CAMPs recommended for students with experience in text-based coding or have attended Exceed Robotics camps in the past. In this camp, students create their own Battlebot with remote controller using CAD software and 3D printing. This camp will teach advanced microcontroller circuits and programming using radio communication. Students will take part in a Battlebot Competition at the end of camp and take their robots home.

What Your Kids Learn ?

Hands-On Robot Design and 3D Printing

Hands-On Robot Design and 3D Printing

During the camp, kids learn to design and 3D print the essential components of a robot capable of holding a pen and moving around to create drawings. Subsequently, they assemble these components to construct their very own drawing robot

Coding and Electronics

Coding and Electronics

The joystick is a popular sensor commonly used to control motor movements. During the camp, students gain proficiency in coding two microcontrollers in the C language: one for the remote joystick and another for the robot

Wireless Communication

Wireless Communication

Wireless control of robots is fascinating for kids. They learn how Radio Frequency (RF) communication is used to connect the joystick and the robot. Additionally, they code the necessary functionality in the C language

Teamwork, Collaboration, Problem Solving

Teamwork, Collaboration, Problem Solving

Some robotics challenges involve teamwork. Kids collaborate, share ideas, and work together to achieve common goals. They learn valuable communication and collaboration skills.
